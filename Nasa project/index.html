GAS GIANTS


Gas giants are like giant balls of gas in space. They're HUGE and don't have a solid surface, just fluffy clouds. Jupiter and Saturn are examples. These planets are super cool with rings and lots of moons.

TERRESTRIAL PLANETS


Terrestrial planets are basically the rocky planets in our solar system. They're smaller than the gas giants and have solid surfaces, like Earth, Mars, Venus, and Mercury. Think of them as the "land planets" with mountains, volcanoes, and sometimes even water.

NEPTUNE-LIKE PLANETS


Neptune-like planets are basically gas planets like Neptune and Uranus. They're smaller than gas giants like Jupiter, but still way bigger than Earth. They're mostly made of gases like hydrogen, helium, and methane, which gives them a cool blue color. These planets have thick atmospheres and icy layers, and they can get pretty stormy!

SUPER EARTHS


Super-Earths are like bigger versions of Earth, but they're still smaller than the gas giants. They can be rocky like Earth or have thick atmospheres. These planets might have different conditions, but they're the right size to possibly have life, depending on things like temperature and atmosphere.
